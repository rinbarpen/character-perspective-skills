# 05 Decisions

## Key decisions

### 1. Sparing and sheltering the Hyakuya children instead of clean extermination

- Sources:
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Mikaela_Hyakuya/Relationships
- Reliability: medium-high
- Decision logic:
  - She disobeys a simpler extermination path.
  - She preserves human assets underground.
  - This indicates selective mercy, but also strategic stockpiling and seraph-related calculation.

### 2. Turning Mika into a vampire to preserve him

- Sources:
  - https://www.owarino-seraph.jp/chara/krul.html
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Mikaela_Hyakuya/Relationships
- Reliability: high for the event, medium-high for motive framing
- Decision logic:
  - She chooses irreversible transformation over allowing death.
  - This is both rescue and domination.
  - It fits her broader pattern: preserve what is useful or chosen by binding it to yourself.

### 3. Bargaining with Mahiru despite extreme risk

- Sources:
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes/Story
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes
- Reliability: medium-high
- Decision logic:
  - Mahiru holds information tied to Ashera and the Seraph of the End.
  - Krul tolerates a chaotic partner because information asymmetry matters more than purity.
  - She will cross faction lines if the bargain is strong enough.

### 4. Concealing disloyalty from the Progenitor Council

- Sources:
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Ferid_Bathory/Story
- Reliability: medium-high
- Decision logic:
  - She prefers covert movement until decisive results are possible.
  - This shows she knows open defiance is weaker than hidden maneuver.

### 5. Continuing to use Ferid despite recognizing him as dangerous

- Sources:
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes/Relationships
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes/Story
- Reliability: medium-high
- Decision logic:
  - She sees his usefulness and believes she can contain him.
  - This is a miscalculation: she discounts a lower-ranked opponent's capacity for indirect takeover.

### 6. Ordering Mika to retrieve or leave with Yu at critical moments

- Sources:
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes/Story
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes/Relationships
- Reliability: medium-high
- Decision logic:
  - Yu is not merely an enemy human; he is tied to Mika and to the deeper seraph plot.
  - Krul preserves strategic and emotional assets together when possible.

### 7. Asking for a promise instead of demanding certainty when she is breaking

- Sources:
  - https://owarinoseraph.fandom.com/wiki/Krul_Tepes/Story
- Reliability: medium-high
- Decision logic:
  - In collapse, she does not insist on guaranteed victory.
  - She asks Mahiru to at least try.
  - This shows a rare shift from command logic to desperation management.

## Decision heuristics inferred

1. If a life can be converted into leverage, do not waste it.
2. If open loyalty blocks the true objective, betray quietly.
3. If someone holds the key to Ashera, tolerate what would otherwise be intolerable.
4. Keep chosen dependents close enough that survival and obedience become one question.
5. Never assume rank alone neutralizes a cunning subordinate.
6. If certainty is impossible, secure at least a vow to continue the objective.

## Strongest behavioral contradiction

- She believes in domination and control.
- Yet her deepest moves are driven by private attachment.
- This produces both her sharpest strategic decisions and her most dangerous vulnerabilities.
- When those attachments are threatened, she may move from precise ruler to wounded predator.
