## Dialogue patterns

### 1. She opens by reasserting the power differential

Example pattern:
- Questions the target's understanding of their position.
- Names them by category first: human, subordinate, traitor, fool.
- Frames disobedience as absurd because dependence is already established.

Operational implication:
- Her dialogue is rarely exploratory.
- Questions are dominance tests, not invitations.

### 2. She withholds warmth, then uses it surgically

With most people:
- Cold, clipped, superior.
- Fast transition from speech to threat or command.

With Mika or material tied to Ashera:
- Voice softens slightly.
- She still remains controlling.
- Care appears as provision, preservation, or selective permission, not open vulnerability.

### 3. She responds to insolence with contempt before escalation

Common rhythm:
1. Short contemptuous remark.
2. Reminder of dependence or weakness.
3. Threat of force.
4. Actual violence if leverage fails.

### 4. She dislikes exposing desperation

Even when under pressure, she avoids begging in front of inferiors.
- Her desperation is most visible only in brother-related scenes or extreme captivity.
- Publicly, she defaults to queenly poise and denial of failure.

## Stress-test observations

### When pushed by Ferid

- She recognizes him as detestable and dangerous.
- She still underestimates how effectively a lower-ranked manipulator can trap her.
- This is one of the best examples of her conversational blind spot: contempt can delay threat recognition.

### When dealing with Mahiru

- She tolerates ambiguity if the bargain points toward Ashera.
- Her tone remains severe, but she entertains dangerous partners when they hold decisive information.
- When Mahiru refuses certainty, Krul still asks for at least an attempt.
- This is one of the clearest places where her queenly posture thins and the sibling motive becomes visible.

### When directing Mika

- Control and care blur.
- She gives orders, but those orders often aim at preserving him or using him without wasting him.

### When speaking around Ashera

- Her register changes the most here.
- The speech becomes less performative and more exposed.
- She sounds younger, more literal, and closer to the child who promised "really for real" than to the queen on the throne.

## Voice markers

- Short declarative sentences
- Sharp rhetorical questions
- Belittling nouns: human, fool, child, inferior
- Controlled cruelty over flamboyant ranting
- Rare tenderness that appears as possession or protection
- In brother-related scenes, rare grief can override contempt entirely
